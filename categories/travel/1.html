Harmony of Hangul

Hangul is the alphabet system of the korean language developed recently in 15th century. Unlilke most of other languages which matured and developed over the years, hangul was created within a short span of time. It was specially created for easy understanding of every citizen and arm them with the greatest weapon and tool called literacy.

The very foundation of the language is very interesting, its harmonious and extensively uses the concept of chinese Yin and yang. Yin signifies negative/darkness while Yang signifies positive/light. Together, compound Yin-Yang in an harmonious way they represent a very philosophical concept of a seeming juxtaposition of two contrasting powers or beings that are interconnected and interdependent. “When you light a candle, you also cast a shadow.”

This harmony is evident in the Hangul-Vowels.

ㆍ: This dot represents the sun

ㅡ: This horizontal line represents the earth

ㅣ: This vertical line represents a man

Yin: Negative/Darkness exists when the sun lies below the earth or behind the man. ㅜ (u) and ㅓ (eo).

Yang: Positive/Light exists when the sun shines above the earth or in front of the man. ㅗ (o) and ㅏ (a).

Neutral: Absence of sun represents the neutral vowels ㅡ (eu/ŭ) ㅣ (i).

Before discussing the harmony of these basic vowels, we introduce the concept of iotized vowels which in simple words is introduction of one more identical stroke for the "sun".

The simple iotated vowels are (Adding of y in english):

ㅑ ya from ㅏ a

ㅕ yeo from ㅓ eo

ㅛ yo from ㅗ o

ㅠ yu from ㅜ u

ㅒ yae from ㅐ ae

ㅖ ye from ㅔ e

Yin vowels and Yang vowels are combined to create compound vowels as follows:

Yin: ㅘ wa = ㅗ o + ㅏ a

Yang: ㅝ wo = ㅜ u + ㅓ eo

Yin: ㅙ wae = ㅗ o + ㅐ ae

Yang: ㅞ we = ㅜ u + ㅔ e

There are 10 basic vowels in Hangul. These are further created into 21 vowels by combining them into diapthongs.

21 of them formed with harmony of the basic vowels are listed below:-

+i

Basic: ㅏ(a) ㅓ(eo) ㅗ(o) ㅜ(u) ㅡ(eu) ㅣ(i)

Add i (+i): ㅐ(ae) ㅔ(e) ㅚ(oe) ㅟ(ui) ㅢ(eui)

Add y (y+): ㅑ(ya) ㅕ(yeo) ㅛ(yo) ㅠ(yu) ㅒ(yae) ㅖ(ye)

Add w (w+): ㅘ(wa) ㅝ(wo) ㅙ(wae) ㅞ(we)

Hangul is definitely interesting. The alphabets are easy to learn, read and understand but building vocabulary and learning grammar overtime is a very daunting task.
